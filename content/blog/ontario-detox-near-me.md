“Ontario detox near me” is one of the most common searches people make when substance use has tipped from worrying into urgent. Caregivers type it for a partner or adult child. People in distress type it for themselves. What Google often shows is a mix of ads, old directories, and clinics that closed or changed their intake months ago.

You do not need a perfect map of every detox bed in the province. You need a live referral.

In Ontario, call **ConnexOntario** at [1-866-531-2600](tel:18665312600) or visit [connexontario.ca](https://connexontario.ca). They help people find mental health, addiction, and crisis services — including withdrawal management (detox) and treatment programs — based on what is available now. Phone, chat, text, and email options are listed on their site.

For health questions that are not an emergency but still feel pressing, Ontario’s **Health 811** line connects you with a registered nurse. Dial **811**. If someone is in immediate danger, call **911**. For suicidal crisis or severe distress, call or text **988**.

A few practical notes while you wait on the line or chat: have the person’s age, city or region, and substances of concern if you know them. Ask whether they need medical detox, residential treatment, or day programs. If they are also housing-unstable, say so — the system often needs both pathways started, not one secret “best” clinic.
