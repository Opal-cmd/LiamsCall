Finding Toronto Shelter Help Without Guessing Phone Numbers

If you typed “Toronto shelter” into a search engine, you are probably looking for something urgent: a bed tonight, a place for someone you love, or a next step when housing feels impossible. Random clinic pages and old phone numbers make that harder, not easier. Shelter vacancies change by the hour. A number that looked right last year can put you through to a closed program.

For Toronto shelter access, start with the city’s own intake path instead of collecting individual shelter lines.

Call Toronto Shelter Central Intake at 416-338-4766 or toll-free 1-877-338-4766. That line is built to place people into the shelter system. You can also review shelter and housing information on the City of Toronto site at toronto.ca (search Shelter System or Central Intake).

If you are already in Toronto and need city information — including housing-related questions — dial 311. For broader social services, food banks, and local housing supports across Ontario, dial 2-1-1 or use 211ontario.ca.

If someone is in immediate danger, call 911. If they are in suicidal crisis or severe distress, call or text 988.

Caregivers often end up doing this search for a sibling, adult child, or parent. It is okay to make the call on their behalf when they will not or cannot. Keep notes: date, who you spoke with, and what they asked you to do next. That one call to Central Intake is usually more useful than five unverified numbers from a blog list.

Liam’s Call cannot book a bed or confirm vacancies. We can help you think through what to say on the call, and point you back to [verified crisis and local resources](/resources). When housing is the urgent issue, get that intake call started before opening ten more tabs.
